DAAD Scholarship Germany Full Guide 2026

Everything you need to know about Master's and PhD funding in Germany.

1. What is the DAAD Scholarship?

The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) is the world's largest funding organization for the international exchange of students and researchers. In 2026, the DAAD continues its mission to support future leaders from developing countries through its "Development-Related Postgraduate Courses" (EPOS) program.

This scholarship is specifically designed for international students who wish to pursue a Master’s or PhD degree at a state or state-recognized German university, focusing on subjects that are relevant to the development of their home countries.

2. Financial Benefits & Coverage

The DAAD scholarship is a fully-funded program, meaning it covers almost every expense a student might face:

  • Monthly Stipend: €934 for Master's students and €1,200 for doctoral candidates.
  • Tuition Fees: Most state universities in Germany are free, but DAAD covers any additional fees if applicable.
  • Insurance: Full health, accident, and personal liability insurance coverage.
  • Travel Allowance: Coverage for flights from your home country to Germany and back.
  • Study & Research Grant: Annual allowance for books and research materials.

3. Eligibility Criteria (In-Depth)

To be a successful applicant, you must meet the following strict requirements:

Academic Background

A Bachelor's degree (typically 4 years) in a relevant subject, completed no more than 6 years before the application date.

Work Experience

Minimum of 2 years of professional work experience in a related field after completing your first degree.

Language Skills

English (IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL 80+) or German (DSH 2 / TestDaF 4) depending on the course requirements.

Commitment

Applicants must show a strong desire to return to their home country to implement the knowledge gained in Germany.

4. Required Documents Checklist

Ensure all documents are translated into English or German by official services:

  • Signed DAAD Application Form.
  • Hand-signed CV (Europass format recommended).
  • Motivation Letter (maximum 2 pages).
  • Academic Transcripts and Degree Certificates.
  • Proof of Employment (Work experience letters).
  • Language Proficiency Certificate.
