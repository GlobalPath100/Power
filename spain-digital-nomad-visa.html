Spain Digital Nomad Visa Requirements (Updated 2026)

Updated for 2026 · Remote Workers · Freelancers · Non-EU Citizens

Spain has become one of the most popular destinations for remote workers and freelancers. With its Mediterranean lifestyle, strong internet infrastructure, and favorable tax regime, the Spain Digital Nomad Visa is one of the best residency options in Europe in 2026.

This guide explains the exact requirements, minimum income, tax benefits, and the full application process step by step.

Who Can Apply for the Spain Digital Nomad Visa?

The visa is designed for non-EU nationals who work remotely using digital technologies.

  • Remote employees working for non-Spanish companies
  • Freelancers with international clients
  • Startup founders and online entrepreneurs
  • IT professionals, designers, marketers, consultants

Spain Digital Nomad Visa Requirements (2026)

  • Valid Passport (minimum 1 year validity)
  • Minimum Monthly Income of approximately €2,160
  • Proof of Remote Work (employment contract or freelance agreements)
  • Health Insurance valid in Spain
  • Clean Criminal Record from the last 5 years
  • University Degree or 3+ years of experience

Income & Tax Benefits

One of the biggest advantages of the Spain Digital Nomad Visa is its tax regime.

  • Flat 15% income tax for up to 4 years
  • Applicable to income up to €600,000
  • No wealth tax for foreign assets (in most cases)

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Prepare all required documents
  2. Apply at a Spanish Consulate or from Spain
  3. Wait for approval (20–45 days on average)
  4. Register address and obtain NIE
  5. Apply for the residence card (TIE)

Common Reasons for Rejection

  • Income below the required threshold
  • Working for Spanish companies
  • Incomplete or untranslated documents
  • Incorrect tax understanding

Final Thoughts

Spain’s Digital Nomad Visa is one of the most attractive visas in Europe in 2026. If you meet the income requirements and work remotely, this visa offers lifestyle, tax benefits, and long-term stability in the EU.
