The Chevening Scholarship Guide 2026

Master's Degrees in the UK for Future Global Leaders.

Introduction to Chevening

Chevening is the UK government’s international awards program aimed at developing global leaders. Funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO), Chevening offers a unique opportunity for future leaders, influencers, and decision-makers from all over the world to develop professionally and academically.

Full Coverage Details

Tuition: Full payment of university tuition fees.

Living: A monthly stipend to cover housing and meals.

Travel: Economy class flights to and from the UK.

Arrival: An arrival allowance and cost of visa application.

Critical Success Factors: The Four Essays

The heart of the Chevening application lies in four mandatory essays (500 words each):

1. Leadership and Influence

You must describe your leadership skills and provide clear examples of how you have influenced others in your career or community.

2. Networking

Focus on how you build and maintain professional relationships and how you plan to use the Chevening network in the future.

3. Studying in the UK

Explain why you chose your three specific Master's courses and how they fit into your academic background.

4. Career Plan

Describe your short-term and long-term career goals and how the scholarship will help you achieve them back home.

Application Timeline 2026

  • August - November: Application window open.
  • February: Shortlisting for interviews.
  • March - May: Interview period at UK embassies.
  • June: Final results announced.
  • September/October: Studies begin in the UK.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who is eligible for Chevening Scholarship?

Applicants must be citizens of eligible countries, hold a bachelor’s degree, have at least 2 years of work experience, and demonstrate leadership potential.

How much does the scholarship cover?

Chevening is fully funded, covering tuition, living stipend, travel costs, visa, and arrival allowance.

Can I apply for multiple universities?

Yes, you can select up to three eligible UK Master’s courses during your application.

Do I need to know English fluently?

You must demonstrate English proficiency via IELTS, TOEFL, or other approved tests as per your course requirement.

Can Chevening scholarship be deferred?

Generally, deferrals are not allowed, but exceptional cases may be considered by the Chevening Secretariat.
