Portugal Digital Nomad Visa Income Requirements

Updated for 2026 · Remote Workers · Freelancers · Non-EU Citizens

Introduction

Portugal is one of Europe’s top destinations for digital nomads thanks to its mild climate, modern infrastructure, safety, and relatively low cost of living.

In 2026, Portugal’s Digital Nomad Visa allows non-EU citizens to legally live in Portugal while working remotely for foreign companies or clients.

Who Can Apply?

  • Remote employees working for non-Portuguese companies
  • Freelancers with international clients
  • Self-employed digital professionals
  • Online entrepreneurs and consultants

Income Requirements (2026)

  • Minimum monthly income: approx. €3,680 net
  • Income must be stable and recurring
  • Must come primarily from outside Portugal
  • Extra income required for dependents

Accepted Proof of Income

  1. Bank statements (last 6–12 months)
  2. Employment or freelance contracts
  3. Invoices and payment receipts
  4. Tax declarations (if available)

Application Process (Step-by-Step)

  1. Prepare all financial and identity documents
  2. Secure accommodation in Portugal
  3. Purchase valid health insurance
  4. Apply through Portuguese consulate or VFS
  5. Attend biometric appointment
  6. Receive visa approval

Processing Time & Validity

  • Processing time: 30–90 days
  • Initial validity: up to 1 year
  • Renewable for up to 2 additional years
  • Possible path to long-term residency

Accommodation & Health Insurance

  • Rental contract or hotel booking required
  • Insurance must cover entire stay
  • Minimum medical coverage: €30,000

Taxes in Portugal

Portugal offers competitive tax options for foreign residents. Some digital nomads may benefit from special tax regimes depending on profession and income structure.

Common Reasons for Rejection

  • Unstable or insufficient income
  • Incomplete documentation
  • Untranslated documents
  • Weak proof of accommodation

FAQ

Is Portuguese language required? No.

Can freelancers apply? Yes.

Can I bring my family? Yes, with higher income proof.

Does it lead to citizenship? Long-term residency may lead to it.
